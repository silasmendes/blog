title = 'Why I am starting a blog again (in the age of AI)'
+++

In this age of AI, YouTube channels, and endless short videos it feels almost… weird to start a blog.

But I am old school :)

I kicked off my career more than 20 years ago as a SQL Server DBA. Back then, starting a blog turned out to be one of the best decisions I ever made. That “Diary of a DBA” played a huge role in my growth. Through it, I learned a ton from the SQL Server community in Brazil, met some truly amazing people, and had the chance to share what I knew with others.

A lot has happened since then. I stopped writing, and somewhere along the way I lost that old blog (which still hurts a little). But writing has always been part of how I learn. When I write, I reinforce ideas. I organize them. I make them stick.

Over the years, I kept writing, just not publicly. First, I stored most of my notes in *Microsoft OneNote*. Later, I migrated heavily to [Obsidian](https://obsidian.md/), mainly because I fell in love with [markdown](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Markdown). Clean and simple :)

And then I realized something: a lot of what I had in my private Obsidian vault was the same stuff I kept sharing with colleagues. Notes, explanations, insights... things that could actually help other people. So why not make it public again?

Yeah... blogs may not be trendy anymore. But they are still powerful. And who knows… maybe some AI out there will get a little smarter (or dumber) from what I write here.
